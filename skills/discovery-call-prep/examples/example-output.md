# Example Output — Discovery Call Prep

Client: Artway (music agency)
Meeting: First discovery call

## 1. Meeting objective

Understand how Artway currently manages artist information, press kits, and booking inquiries, and identify the real operational pain behind the stated website problem.

## 2. Client summary

Artway is a Portuguese music agency representing artists. Their public site lists artist profiles, and press kits appear to be shared as Google Drive links rather than through a structured press kit system.

## 3. Current understanding

- Artists are represented and listed on the public site.
- Press kit experience is informal.
- It is unclear whether the website pain reflects website limitations or deeper operational gaps in how artist data is collected, approved, and published.

## 4. Likely operational bottlenecks

- Manual back-and-forth collecting artist materials.
- No structured internal artist profile workflow.
- Booking inquiries possibly tracked across email and chat with no shared view.
- Approval flow before publishing artist updates is unclear.

## 5. Discovery questions

### Business model & customers
- Who pays Artway, and for what?
- How are artists onboarded?

### Operations & workflows
- Walk us through how an artist's profile gets updated today, from request to live.
- How do you collect press kit materials from artists?
- Where do those materials live?

### Tools & systems
- Which tools do you actively use? Which do you wish you didn't?
- Is there a CMS the team can edit directly, or do you depend on a developer?

### People & decisions
- Who approves what goes on the website?
- Who answers booking inquiries today, and how fast?

### Commercial & timeline
- Is there a deadline or event driving the urgency?
- What would success look like 12 months from now?

## 6. Possible opportunity areas (honest)

- Structured artist profile system that the team can update directly.
- Press kit workflow with templated fields and approvals.
- Booking inquiry intake with a single shared view.

Each of these depends on what we learn in discovery. None promised before facts.

## 7. Risks and assumptions

- We assume the website problem is the visible part of a deeper ops problem. To be confirmed.
- We assume no central CRM exists. To be confirmed.
- Risk: the client only wants a website refresh, not an ops project. Be explicit about which is which.

## 8. Recommended meeting angle

Lead with operations, not features. Ask how artist information flows through the team before discussing what the website should look like. Listen for spreadsheet, WhatsApp, and email patterns — those are the real signals.
