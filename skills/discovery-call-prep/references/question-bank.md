# Question Bank — Discovery

A pool of high-yield discovery questions. Pull 4–7 per group, tailored to the prospect. Don't ask all of these in one call — the goal is depth, not coverage.

## Business model & customers

- Walk me through how your business actually makes money — biggest revenue line first.
- Who is your typical customer? Has that changed in the last 12 months?
- What channels bring you the most bookings / clients today? Which are growing? Which are declining?
- How do you handle seasonality? Where do peaks and troughs hurt the most?
- Where do you compete on price, and where do you compete on experience?
- If a customer churns or doesn't return, do you know why?

## Operations & workflows

- Walk me through one full cycle — from first inquiry to post-sale — as if I'd never seen it.
- Where in that flow do mistakes happen most? What does a mistake look like?
- Which handoff between people / tools is the most fragile?
- If you had to rebuild one process from scratch, which would it be and why?
- What does a bad day look like operationally? What broke?
- Which tasks does your team complain about most?
- How do you handle exceptions — refunds, no-shows, last-minute changes, complaints?

## Tools & systems

- What tools are you actively using? (List them, even the ugly ones.)
- Which tool is the team most reluctant to use, and why?
- Which tool would you remove tomorrow if you could?
- Where do you still rely on spreadsheets or WhatsApp / email threads?
- Where does data live in two places at once? How do you keep them in sync?
- Who at the company has admin access to those tools? Who's the de-facto IT person?
- Have you tried a tool that didn't stick? Why?

## Booking-specific (if relevant — tours, boats, experiences)

- How do customers book today — direct, OTA, walk-up, phone?
- Do you have real-time availability across channels, or do you manage it manually?
- How are you handling payments, deposits, refunds, vouchers?
- Have you had a double-booking in the last 90 days? What happened?
- How do you communicate with customers before, during, and after the experience?
- How are no-shows tracked? Does the team agree on the number?

## CRM / CMS / customer comms

- Where does customer information live? Can you pull a list of last quarter's customers right now?
- How do you re-engage past customers? When was the last campaign?
- Who writes and updates content on your website? How often?
- What's the gap between what you offer and what your website says you offer?
- If a customer emails / DMs / WhatsApps you, who responds? How fast?

## People & decisions

- Who else needs to be in the room before a decision like this gets made?
- Who has been burned by a software project before, and what happened?
- Who on the team would champion a change like this? Who would resist?
- If we built this, who'd own it day-to-day after we leave?
- How does your team feel about AI / automation today — excited, anxious, neutral?

## Commercial & timeline

- What does success look like 12 months from now? In numbers if possible.
- Have you priced out an internal hire instead? What did that look like?
- What's your budget posture — investment mode, pilot mode, or "just curious"?
- Is there a season, event, or deadline driving urgency? Or is this fully discretionary timing?
- If we agreed today, when would you want to see first value? Six weeks? Six months?
- What would make this project a failure in your eyes?

## "Real problem" probes (use sparingly, late in the call)

- Of everything we've talked about, what would actually move the needle for you?
- What have you tried before that didn't work, and why?
- If you do nothing for the next 12 months, what happens?
- Be honest — is the real driver here cost, time, growth, or something else?
