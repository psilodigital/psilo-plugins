# Discovery Call Prep

## When to use

Use this skill when preparing for:
- client meetings
- discovery calls
- exploratory calls
- sales consultations
- business process interviews

## Inputs

- Client name
- Website or business description
- Known context
- Meeting goal
- Relevant Psilodigital services
- Any previous notes

## Procedure

1. Identify the client's business model.
2. Identify likely operational bottlenecks.
3. Separate website-level issues from internal operations issues.
4. Identify what is fact and what is assumption.
5. Generate discovery questions.
6. Group questions by business area.
7. Suggest opportunity areas without overpromising.
8. Produce a meeting brief.

## Output format

Return:

1. Meeting objective
2. Client summary
3. Current understanding
4. Likely operational bottlenecks
5. Discovery questions
6. Possible opportunity areas
7. Risks and assumptions
8. Recommended meeting angle

## Rules

- Do not jump straight to solutions.
- Do not overpromise integrations.
- Use business-friendly language.
- Make assumptions explicit.
- Focus on understanding operations before selling.
