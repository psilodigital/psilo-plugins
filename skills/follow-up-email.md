# Skill: follow-up-email

**Purpose:** Draft a follow-up email that moves the deal or project forward without being pushy.

## When to use

- After a discovery / pitch / scoping call
- After sending a proposal (if no reply in 5–7 days)
- After a milestone demo
- After a project goes live (handover + retainer offer)

## Inputs

- Client name + recipient
- Context (what happened in the meeting / what was sent)
- Desired next step (single, specific)
- Tone (default: friendly + direct; can be "warmer" or "more formal" on request)

## Output

Save to `vault/clients/<client>/communications/<YYYY-MM-DD>-followup.md` as a draft. **Never send directly.**

```
Subject: <short, specific>

Hi <name>,

<1 sentence: reference the conversation specifically — not "great chatting!">

<1–2 sentences: what we heard and what stood out.>

<1 paragraph: what we recommend / what's attached.>

<Clear next step. One ask. Date.>

Cheers,
<Operator>
```

## Patterns

### After discovery
- Reference 2 specific things they said (proves we listened).
- Restate the problem in their words.
- Propose the next step (paid discovery, free design sprint, proposal, etc.).

### After proposal (gentle nudge)
- Don't apologize for following up.
- Add value: a relevant case, a small piece of insight, a clarification we forgot.
- Single question to unstick the decision ("is timing or scope the bigger concern?").

### After milestone demo
- Summarize what we shipped.
- Show what's next.
- Flag any decision needed.

### After go-live
- Thank, congratulate, recap outcomes.
- Offer retainer or support option.
- Ask for a testimonial if it makes sense.

## Guardrails

- **No fake urgency.** No "circling back" or "checking in" without substance.
- **One CTA.** Never two.
- **Plain English.** Read aloud — if it sounds like marketing, rewrite it.
- **Send approval:** all client emails require operator sign-off (`governance/human-in-the-loop.md`).
