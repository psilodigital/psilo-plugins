# Proposal Writer

## When to use

Use this skill when creating:
- proposal drafts
- scope proposals
- board-friendly documents
- service offers
- project options

## Inputs

- Client context
- Meeting notes
- Known problems
- Proposed solution
- Scope boundaries
- Pricing assumptions
- Timeline assumptions

## Procedure

1. Summarize the business problem.
2. Reframe the problem in operational terms.
3. Present a practical proposed approach.
4. Separate core scope from optional scope.
5. Define assumptions and exclusions.
6. Define phases.
7. Suggest next steps.
8. Keep tone clear and non-hype.

## Output format

Return:

1. Executive summary
2. Current situation
3. Business challenges
4. Proposed solution
5. Scope
6. Optional additions
7. Timeline
8. Assumptions
9. Investment / pricing placeholder
10. Next steps

## Rules

- Do not invent prices unless requested.
- Do not guarantee outcomes.
- Keep the language client-friendly.
- Make technical detail secondary.
